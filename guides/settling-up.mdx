## Overview

EquiShare automatically calculates optimal settlements to minimize the number of transactions needed to settle all debts within a group. Instead of tracking individual expense-to-expense payments, the app computes net balances and suggests the most efficient payment plan.

<Info>
  Settlements are recalculated automatically whenever expenses are added, deleted, or marked as settled. You always see the current optimal payment plan.
</Info>

## Understanding Settlements

### How Settlement Calculation Works

When you track expenses in EquiShare, the app:

1. **Calculates net balances** for each person
   - Adds up everything they paid
   - Subtracts everything they owe
   - Determines if they should receive money (positive balance) or pay money (negative balance)

2. **Optimizes payment flow**
   - Minimizes the number of transactions needed
   - Creates direct settlements between people
   - Reduces complex chains of payments

3. **Updates in real-time**
   - Recalculates after each new expense
   - Adjusts when expenses are deleted
   - Updates when settlements are marked as paid

### Settlement States

**Unsettled (Active)**
- Outstanding payments that need to be made
- Shown at the top of the settlements list
- Highlighted for easy visibility
- Ready to be marked as paid

**Settled (Paid)**
- Payments that have been completed
- Moved to settlement history
- No longer included in calculations
- Kept for record-keeping

## Viewing Who Owes Whom

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Your Group">
    Open the group where you want to review settlements.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the Settlements Section">
    The settlements area shows all current payment obligations.
    
    Each settlement displays:
    - **From**: Person who needs to pay
    - **To**: Person who should receive payment
    - **Amount**: How much needs to be transferred
    - **Status**: Unsettled or Settled
    
    <Tip>
      Unsettled payments appear first, making it easy to see what needs action.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Understand Your Balance">
    Your personal balance tells you:
    - **Positive balance**: Others owe you money
    - **Negative balance**: You owe money to others
    - **Zero balance**: You're all settled up
    
    The app shows exactly who you need to pay or who needs to pay you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Marking Settlements as Paid

When someone makes a payment, record it in EquiShare to keep balances accurate.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete the Payment">
    Make the actual payment outside EquiShare:
    - Bank transfer
    - Cash
    - Payment apps (UPI, PayTm, etc.)
    - Any method agreed upon by the group
    
    <Warning>
      EquiShare doesn't process payments. It only tracks who owes whom. You must handle actual money transfers separately.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Mark as Settled in EquiShare">
    After the payment is complete:
    1. Find the corresponding settlement in the list
    2. Click **Mark as Paid** or the checkmark button
    3. Confirm the action
    
    The settlement will:
    - Move to your settlement history
    - Be marked as paid
    - Stop appearing in unsettled payments
    - Trigger a recalculation of remaining settlements
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify Updated Balances">
    After marking a settlement as paid, check that:
    - Your balance updated correctly
    - Other settlements adjusted if needed
    - The settlement appears in your history
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Settle All at Once

If all debts in a group have been paid, use the **Settle All** button:

1. Click **Settle All** in the settlements section
2. Confirm that all payments have been made
3. All unsettled payments move to history
4. Everyone's balance returns to zero

<Warning>
  Only use Settle All when all payments are actually complete. This action marks ALL unsettled payments as paid.
</Warning>

## Settlement History

EquiShare maintains a complete history of all settlements.

### Viewing Past Settlements

The settlement history shows:
- All previously settled payments
- When each settlement was marked as paid
- Which expense(s) contributed to each settlement
- The amount that was settled

### Why Settlement History Matters

**Record Keeping**
- Proof of who paid whom and when
- Useful for tax purposes or record-keeping
- Helps resolve disputes

**Transparency**
- Everyone can see the complete payment history
- Builds trust in the group
- Makes it easy to verify past transactions

**Reference**
- Look back at how expenses were settled
- Understand payment patterns over time
- Review completed transactions

## Real-World Examples

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Example 1: Simple Three-Person Split">
    **The Situation:**
    - Alex paid ₹3,000 for a hotel
    - Beth paid ₹1,200 for gas
    - Carlos paid ₹600 for food
    - Total expenses: ₹4,800
    - Each person's fair share: ₹1,600
    
    **Net Balances:**
    - Alex: Paid ₹3,000, owes ₹1,600 = **+₹1,400** (should receive)
    - Beth: Paid ₹1,200, owes ₹1,600 = **-₹400** (should pay)
    - Carlos: Paid ₹600, owes ₹1,600 = **-₹1,000** (should pay)
    
    **Optimal Settlements:**
    - Beth pays Alex ₹400
    - Carlos pays Alex ₹1,000
    
    **Result:** Just 2 transactions settle everyone (instead of potentially 6 if tracking every expense individually).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Example 2: Unequal Expense Sharing">
    **The Situation:**
    - 4 roommates sharing apartment costs
    - Rent ₹32,000 split as 40%/30%/20%/10% based on room sizes
    - Electricity ₹2,000 split equally
    - Person A paid both bills (₹34,000 total)
    
    **What Each Person Owes:**
    - Person A: 40% of ₹32,000 + 25% of ₹2,000 = ₹13,300 (but paid ₹34,000)
    - Person B: 30% of ₹32,000 + 25% of ₹2,000 = ₹10,100
    - Person C: 20% of ₹32,000 + 25% of ₹2,000 = ₹6,900
    - Person D: 10% of ₹32,000 + 25% of ₹2,000 = ₹3,700
    
    **Net Balances:**
    - Person A: **+₹20,700** (should receive)
    - Person B: **-₹10,100** (should pay)
    - Person C: **-₹6,900** (should pay)
    - Person D: **-₹3,700** (should pay)
    
    **Optimal Settlements:**
    - Person B pays Person A ₹10,100
    - Person C pays Person A ₹6,900
    - Person D pays Person A ₹3,700
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Example 3: Complex Multi-Expense Trip">
    **The Situation:**
    - 5 friends on a 3-day trip
    - 12 different expenses
    - Everyone paid for different things at different times
    - Some expenses split equally, others split by who participated
    
    **Without EquiShare:**
    - Would need to track each expense individually
    - Potentially 30+ individual transactions to settle
    - Easy to lose track of who owes what
    
    **With EquiShare:**
    - App calculates net balances automatically
    - Suggests 3-4 optimal payments
    - Everyone knows exactly what they owe or are owed
    - Simple, clear settlement process
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Understanding the Settlement Algorithm

EquiShare uses a greedy algorithm to minimize transactions:

1. **Calculate net balances** for everyone
2. **Identify creditors** (people who should receive money)
3. **Identify debtors** (people who should pay money)
4. **Match largest debtor with largest creditor**
5. **Repeat** until all balances are zero

This approach ensures the minimum number of transactions while settling all debts.

<Info>
  The algorithm always produces the same result for a given set of expenses, ensuring consistency and fairness.
</Info>

## API Reference

For developers working with settlements:

- `settlement.list` - Get all settlements for a group
- `settlement.settle` - Mark a single settlement as paid
- `settlement.settleAll` - Mark all settlements as paid
- `settlement.recompute` - Manually trigger recalculation

See the [Settlement Router API](/api/routers/settlement) for complete details.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Settlement calculations seem wrong">
    Settlement calculations are based on net balances, not individual expenses. The app optimizes for minimum transactions, so the person you pay might not be the person you shared a specific expense with.
    
    To verify:
    1. Check your net balance
    2. Add up all you paid
    3. Subtract all you owe
    4. The difference should match your balance
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can't mark a settlement as paid">
    Ensure:
    - You have access to the group (owner or member)
    - The settlement exists and is unsettled
    - You're connected to the internet
    - You have the necessary permissions
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Settlement disappeared after marking as paid">
    This is expected behavior. Settled payments move to settlement history. Check the history section to see past settlements.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Need to undo a settled payment">
    You cannot directly undo a settled payment. If marked incorrectly:
    1. Add a correcting expense to adjust balances
    2. Or contact your group owner to delete and recreate relevant expenses
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
