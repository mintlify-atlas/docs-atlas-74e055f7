## Overview

Groups are the foundation of expense sharing in EquiShare. A group brings together people who share expenses, whether it's roommates splitting rent, friends on a trip, or colleagues organizing team lunches.

<Note>
  You must be signed in to create a group. Groups you create are automatically added to your dashboard.
</Note>

## Creating Your First Group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Create Group Dialog">
    From your dashboard, click the **Create New Group** button. This opens a dialog where you'll configure your new group.
    
    <Tip>
      You can create as many groups as you need. There's no limit to the number of groups you can manage.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter a Group Name">
    Choose a descriptive name for your group. Good examples include:
    
    - "Weekend Trip to Goa"
    - "Apartment 3B Roommates"
    - "Office Lunch Club"
    - "Family Vacation 2026"
    
    The group name helps you quickly identify the group in your dashboard, especially when you're managing multiple groups.
    
    <Info>
      Group names can be changed later if needed, so don't worry about getting it perfect the first time.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add People (Optional)">
    You can add initial members to your group during creation. Enter each person's name and click the plus button or press **Enter** to add them.
    
    **Adding people:**
    1. Type a person's name in the input field
    2. Press **Enter** or click the **+** button
    3. Repeat for each additional person
    4. Remove anyone by clicking the **X** on their badge
    
    <Tip>
      You can add people later, so it's okay to start with just yourself or a few people and invite others afterward.
    </Tip>
    
    <Warning>
      Make sure names are spelled correctly and consistently. Each person should have one unique entry to ensure accurate expense tracking.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create the Group">
    Once you've entered the group name (and optionally added people), click **Create Group**. You'll be automatically redirected to your new group's page.
    
    The system will:
    - Create the group with you as the owner
    - Add all specified people to the group
    - Initialize the group with zero expenses and zero settlements
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding Group Roles

EquiShare has two types of group participants:

### Group Owner
- Creates and owns the group
- Can delete the group
- Can add or remove members
- Can add or remove people (non-registered participants)
- Has full access to all group features

### Group Members
- Added via invite links
- Can view all expenses and settlements
- Can add expenses
- Can mark settlements as paid
- Cannot delete the group
- Can leave the group at any time

<Info>
  "People" are participants in expenses (they can be added by name without registration). "Members" are registered users who have accepted an invite to join the group.
</Info>

## Managing People in Your Group

After creating a group, you can manage the people who participate in expenses.

### Adding More People

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Group Settings">
    From your group's page, access the people management section.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add New Person">
    Enter the person's name and add them to the group. They'll immediately be available when creating expenses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Removing People

<Warning>
  You cannot remove a person who has been part of any expense or settlement. This prevents data inconsistencies and maintains accurate financial records.
</Warning>

To remove a person who hasn't been part of any expenses:
1. Click the remove button next to their name
2. Confirm the deletion

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use Descriptive Group Names">
    Choose names that clearly indicate the group's purpose. Include dates for time-specific events ("Summer 2026 Road Trip") or locations for ongoing shared expenses ("Downtown Apartment - Unit 42").
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Add All Known Participants Upfront">
    If you know everyone who'll share expenses, add them during group creation. This saves time later and ensures everyone is ready when you start tracking expenses.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use Consistent Names">
    Always use the same name format for each person. If you start with "John Smith," don't later use "John" or "J. Smith" - pick one format and stick with it.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Create Separate Groups for Different Contexts">
    Don't mix unrelated expenses. Create separate groups for:
    - Different trips or events
    - Different households
    - Different friend groups
    - Work expenses vs. personal expenses
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Real-World Examples

### Example 1: Weekend Trip

**Scenario:** You're planning a weekend getaway with 4 friends.

**Setup:**
1. Create group: "Beach Weekend - March 2026"
2. Add people: Sarah, Mike, Emma, Chris
3. Start tracking expenses immediately

### Example 2: Shared Apartment

**Scenario:** Three roommates sharing rent and utilities.

**Setup:**
1. Create group: "Apartment 5C - Oak Street"
2. Add people: Alex, Jordan, Casey
3. Use for recurring monthly expenses

### Example 3: Office Team Lunches

**Scenario:** Your team takes turns buying lunch for everyone.

**Setup:**
1. Create group: "Marketing Team Lunches"
2. Add all team members
3. Track who paid and split costs equally

## API Reference

For developers integrating with EquiShare, see the [Group Router API](/api/routers/group) documentation for details on:
- `group.create` - Create a new group
- `group.addPerson` - Add people to existing groups
- `group.deletePerson` - Remove people from groups

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can't find the Create Group button">
    Make sure you're signed in to your account. The Create Group button appears on your dashboard after authentication.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Group creation fails">
    - Ensure you've entered a group name (this field is required)
    - Check your internet connection
    - Try refreshing the page and attempting again
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can't remove a person from the group">
    People who have participated in any expense or settlement cannot be removed. This is intentional to maintain data integrity. You can create a new group if needed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
