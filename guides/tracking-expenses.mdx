## Overview

Expense tracking is at the heart of EquiShare. The app provides flexible splitting methods to handle any type of shared cost, from equal splits to custom allocations.

<Info>
  All expenses are automatically included in settlement calculations. When you add or remove an expense, settlements update in real-time.
</Info>

## Adding an Expense

The expense form uses a two-step process to make data entry quick and accurate.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Expense Form">
    From your group's page, click **Add Expense**. This opens a dialog with a two-step form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Expense Details (Step 1)">
    Provide the basic information about the expense:
    
    **Required fields:**
    - **Description**: What was the expense? (e.g., "Dinner at restaurant", "Grocery shopping", "Hotel booking")
    - **Amount**: Total cost in rupees (₹)
    - **Paid by**: Select who paid for this expense
    - **Split between**: Select all people who should share this cost
    
    <Tip>
      Write clear descriptions that you'll recognize later. Include the date or location if helpful ("Coffee on 3/15" or "Uber to airport").
    </Tip>
    
    <Warning>
      Make sure the amount is accurate before continuing. While you can delete and recreate expenses, it's easier to get it right the first time.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure the Split (Step 2)">
    Choose how to divide the expense among participants. EquiShare offers three split methods:
    
    ### Equal Split
    Divides the total amount equally among selected people.
    - **Use when**: Everyone should pay the same amount
    - **Example**: Splitting a restaurant bill among friends
    - **Calculation**: ₹1,200 ÷ 4 people = ₹300 per person
    
    ### Percent Split
    Assigns a percentage of the total to each person.
    - **Use when**: Different people should pay different proportions
    - **Example**: Splitting rent where one person has the larger bedroom
    - **Percentages must total 100%**
    - Use the sliders to adjust each person's share
    - Click **Auto Balance** to distribute remaining percentage equally
    
    ### Exact Amount Split
    Specifies an exact amount for each person.
    - **Use when**: You know exactly what each person owes
    - **Example**: Everyone ordered different items with different prices
    - **Amounts must total the expense amount**
    - Enter the specific amount for each person
    - Click **Auto Balance** to distribute remaining amount equally
    
    <Info>
      The form shows real-time totals to help ensure your split adds up correctly. Look for the green checkmark when totals match.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit the Expense">
    Review your entries and click **Add Expense**. The system will:
    - Create the expense record
    - Calculate individual shares based on your split configuration
    - Update everyone's balances
    - Recalculate optimal settlements for the group
    - Notify you of success
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Split Methods in Detail

### Equal Split - The Simple Way

Best for shared expenses where everyone benefits equally.

**Example: Group Dinner**
- Total bill: ₹2,400
- People: Alex, Beth, Carlos, Diana (4 people)
- Split: ₹2,400 ÷ 4 = ₹600 each

**Pros:**
- Fastest method
- No calculations needed
- Fair for truly shared expenses

**Cons:**
- Not suitable when people consume different amounts

### Percent Split - Proportional Sharing

Perfect for expenses where contribution varies by agreed ratios.

**Example: Shared Apartment Rent**
- Total rent: ₹30,000
- Roommate A (master bedroom): 50%
- Roommate B (regular room): 30%
- Roommate C (regular room): 20%

**Share calculation:**
- Roommate A: ₹30,000 × 50% = ₹15,000
- Roommate B: ₹30,000 × 30% = ₹9,000
- Roommate C: ₹30,000 × 20% = ₹6,000

**Pros:**
- Handles unequal splits cleanly
- Easy to adjust ratios with sliders
- Percentages scale with any amount

**Cons:**
- Requires agreeing on percentage splits
- May create decimal amounts

### Exact Amount Split - Precise Control

Ideal when you know exactly what each person owes.

**Example: Grocery Shopping**
- Total: ₹1,500
- Person A bought personal items worth ₹800
- Person B bought personal items worth ₹500
- Shared items worth ₹200 (₹100 each)

**Share calculation:**
- Person A: ₹800 (personal) + ₹100 (shared) = ₹900
- Person B: ₹500 (personal) + ₹100 (shared) = ₹600

**Pros:**
- Most accurate for mixed purchases
- No ambiguity about amounts
- Works for itemized bills

**Cons:**
- Takes longer to enter
- Requires knowing exact amounts

## Using the Split Configuration Tools

### Auto Balance Button

The **Auto Balance** feature helps when your split doesn't add up correctly.

**For Percent Split:**
- Distributes remaining percentage equally among all participants
- Example: If you've assigned 80%, Auto Balance splits the remaining 20% equally

**For Exact Split:**
- Distributes remaining amount equally among all participants
- Example: If you've assigned ₹800 of ₹1,000, Auto Balance adds ₹100 to each person

### Real-Time Validation

The form continuously validates your split:
- **Green indicator**: Your split is valid and matches the total
- **Orange/Red indicator**: Your split doesn't match - adjust or use Auto Balance
- **Error message**: Shows exactly what needs fixing

## Managing Expense History

### Viewing Expenses

All expenses appear in your group's expense list, showing:
- Description and amount
- Who paid
- How it was split
- Current settlement status

### Deleting Expenses

To remove an incorrect expense:
1. Find the expense in the list
2. Click the delete button
3. Confirm deletion

<Warning>
  Deleting an expense immediately recalculates all settlements. Make sure this is intentional before confirming.
</Warning>

## Best Practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Enter Expenses Promptly" icon="clock">
    Add expenses as they happen or daily. This prevents forgotten expenses and keeps everyone informed.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Be Descriptive" icon="pen">
    Include enough detail so anyone can understand the expense later. "Dinner" is vague; "Dinner at Marina Cafe on March 3rd" is clear.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Verify Amounts" icon="calculator">
    Double-check amounts before submitting. Small errors accumulate and cause confusion later.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Choose the Right Split Method" icon="chart-pie">
    Use equal splits when fair, but don't force equal splits when exact or percent splits are more appropriate.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## API Reference

For developers integrating expense tracking:

- `expense.create` - Create a new expense with shares
- `expense.delete` - Remove an expense
- `expense.getBalances` - Get current balance for all group members

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Split validation error">
    - **Percent Split**: Ensure percentages total exactly 100%
    - **Exact Split**: Ensure amounts total the expense amount
    - Use the **Auto Balance** button to automatically fix small discrepancies
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can't select a person">
    Make sure the person exists in your group. You may need to add them first in the group's people management section.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Expense not appearing">
    - Check that you clicked the final submit button
    - Refresh the page to see updated data
    - Verify you're looking at the correct group
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Need to modify an expense">
    EquiShare doesn't support editing expenses directly. Delete the incorrect expense and create a new one with the correct information.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
