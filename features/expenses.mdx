## Overview

Expenses are the core transactions in EquiShare. Each expense represents a cost that was paid by one person but should be shared among multiple people. EquiShare offers flexible splitting options to handle any real-world scenario.

## Adding an Expense

To add a new expense to a group:

1. Navigate to your group page
2. Click the **Add Expense** button
3. Complete the expense details (Step 1)
4. Configure how to split the cost (Step 2)
5. Submit the expense

### Step 1: Expense Details

Provide the basic information about the expense:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Description">
    Enter what the expense was for (e.g., "Dinner at Restaurant", "Hotel Booking", "Groceries")
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Amount">
    Enter the total amount in rupees (₹). Include decimals for precise amounts.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Paid By">
    Select who paid for this expense from the group members.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Split Among">
    Select all people who should share this cost. You can select multiple people.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  All fields in Step 1 are required before you can proceed to configure the split.
</Note>

### Step 2: Split Configuration

Choose how to divide the expense among the selected people:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Equal Split">
    ## Equal Split
    
    The expense is divided equally among all selected people.
    
    **Example**: A ₹900 dinner split equally among 3 people = ₹300 per person
    
    **When to use**:
    - Restaurant bills where everyone ordered similarly
    - Shared accommodations
    - Group activities with equal participation
    
    **How it works**:
    - The total amount is divided by the number of people
    - Each person owes exactly the same amount
    - No additional configuration needed
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Percentage Split">
    ## Percentage Split
    
    Each person pays a specific percentage of the total.
    
    **Example**: A ₹1000 expense split 50% / 30% / 20% among three people
    
    **When to use**:
    - Unequal participation in the expense
    - Based on agreed-upon ratios (e.g., 70/30 split)
    - When you know the proportions but not exact amounts
    
    **How it works**:
    - Use the slider to adjust each person's percentage
    - The system shows the current total percentage
    - Click **Auto Balance** to redistribute to 100%
    - Total must equal 100% to save the expense
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Exact Amount">
    ## Exact Amount Split
    
    Each person pays a specific fixed amount.
    
    **Example**: A ₹1000 expense where Person A pays ₹400, Person B pays ₹350, Person C pays ₹250
    
    **When to use**:
    - People ordered different amounts at a restaurant
    - Some items were for specific individuals
    - You have exact amounts from an itemized bill
    
    **How it works**:
    - Enter the exact amount for each person
    - The system shows the current total
    - Click **Auto Balance** to distribute any remainder
    - Total must equal the expense amount to save
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Split Configuration Tools

### Auto Balance

The Auto Balance feature helps when your splits don't add up correctly:

- **For Percentage**: Redistributes percentages equally to reach 100%
- **For Exact Amount**: Adjusts amounts proportionally to match the total

### Visual Feedback

The interface provides real-time feedback:

- **Green indicators**: Splits are correctly balanced
- **Orange indicators**: Splits don't add up correctly
- **Current totals**: Always visible at the top

### Adjusting Splits

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Percentage">
    - Use the slider for quick adjustments
    - Slider ranges from 0% to 100%
    - Adjust in 0.5% increments for precision
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Exact Amount">
    - Type the exact amount in the input field
    - Use decimal points for precise amounts
    - Maximum is the total expense amount
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Viewing Expenses

All expenses appear in the group's expense list, showing:

- **Description**: What the expense was for
- **Amount**: Total cost in rupees
- **Paid By**: Who paid initially
- **Split Details**: How it was divided
- **Date**: When the expense was added

### Expense List Features

- Expenses are sorted by date (newest first)
- Each expense shows who participated
- Visual indicators show the payer
- Click an expense to view full split details

## Deleting Expenses

You can delete an expense if:

- You're the group owner or a member with access
- The expense hasn't been settled yet

<Warning>
  Deleting an expense permanently removes it and recalculates all settlements. This action cannot be undone.
</Warning>

### How to Delete

1. Navigate to the expense in the list
2. Click the delete icon or option
3. Confirm the deletion
4. Settlements are automatically recalculated

<Info>
  When you delete an expense, EquiShare automatically recomputes who owes whom based on the remaining expenses.
</Info>

## How Expenses Affect Settlements

Every expense impacts the group's balances:

### Balance Calculation

For each expense:
- The **payer** receives credit for the full amount
- Each **participant** gets debited for their share

**Example**:
```
Expense: ₹900 dinner
Paid by: Alice
Split: Equally among Alice, Bob, Carol (₹300 each)

Balance changes:
- Alice: +₹900 (paid) -₹300 (her share) = +₹600
- Bob: -₹300 (his share)
- Carol: -₹300 (her share)

Result: Bob and Carol each owe Alice ₹300
```

### Automated Settlement Updates

Whenever you add or delete an expense:

1. All balances are recalculated
2. Settlements are recomputed using the minimal transaction algorithm
3. The optimal payment plan is generated
4. Previous settlements remain marked as paid in history

Learn more about this on the [Settlements](/features/settlements) page.

## Best Practices

### Descriptive Names

<Tip>
  Use clear, specific descriptions that will make sense weeks later: "Pizza Hut dinner on Friday" instead of just "Food".
</Tip>

### Timely Entry

Add expenses as they happen:
- Details are fresh in everyone's mind
- Receipts are still available
- Less chance of forgetting expenses

### Choosing Split Methods

**Use Equal Split when**:
- Everyone benefited equally
- Simplicity is more important than precision
- The amounts are roughly similar

**Use Percentage Split when**:
- You have agreed-upon ratios (e.g., roommates by room size)
- You know proportions but not exact amounts
- The split isn't equal but you don't need precision

**Use Exact Amount when**:
- You have an itemized bill
- Different people consumed different amounts
- Precision matters (business expenses)

### Accuracy

- Double-check amounts before submitting
- Include all participants who should share the cost
- Round to 2 decimal places for currency accuracy
- Use Auto Balance to handle rounding differences

## Common Scenarios

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Restaurant Bill with Different Orders">
    **Scenario**: Group dinner where people ordered different amounts
    
    **Solution**: Use Exact Amount split
    - Add the expense with the total bill amount
    - Enter each person's exact cost from the itemized bill
    - Let them add up to the total (including tip and tax)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Shared Rent by Room Size">
    **Scenario**: 3 roommates with different room sizes
    
    **Solution**: Use Percentage split
    - Calculate percentages based on square footage
    - Example: 40% / 35% / 25% split
    - Use the same percentages each month
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Group Grocery Shopping">
    **Scenario**: Buying shared household items
    
    **Solution**: Use Equal split
    - Add the total grocery amount
    - Split equally among household members
    - Simple and fair for common items
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Partial Participation">
    **Scenario**: Not everyone participated in this expense
    
    **Solution**: Select only participants
    - In "Split Among", choose only people who should pay
    - Don't include people who weren't there
    - Use any split method appropriate for the group
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Validation and Error Handling

### Required Fields

You must provide:
- Description (cannot be empty)
- Amount (must be positive)
- Payer (must be from the group)
- Participants (at least one person)

### Split Validation

Before saving, the system validates:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Equal Split">
    - Automatic validation (always valid)
    - No manual adjustment needed
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Percentage Split">
    - Total must equal 100%
    - Tolerance: ±0.01% for rounding
    - Error shown if total is off
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Exact Amount">
    - Total must equal expense amount
    - Tolerance: ±0.01 rupees for rounding
    - Error shown if totals don't match
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Common Validation Errors

| Error | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| "Total shares must equal 100%" | Percentages don't add up | Use Auto Balance or adjust manually |
| "Total shares must equal expense amount" | Exact amounts don't match total | Check your math or use Auto Balance |
| "All share participants must belong to the group" | Selected invalid person | Choose only from group members |
| "Payer must be a person in this group" | Invalid payer selected | Select someone from the group |

## Advanced Tips

### Handling Tips and Taxes

Include tips and taxes in the total amount:

1. Calculate the final total (bill + tip + tax)
2. Enter this as the expense amount
3. Split as appropriate

### Splitting with Non-Payers

If someone should pay but didn't:

1. Choose the actual payer in "Paid By"
2. Include all people who should share the cost in "Split Among"
3. The system tracks who owes the payer

### Multiple Currencies

While EquiShare displays in rupees (₹):

1. Convert foreign amounts to rupees before entering
2. Use the exchange rate at the time of expense
3. Note the original currency in the description if needed
