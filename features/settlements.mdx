## Overview

Settlements in EquiShare represent the actual payments needed to balance all debts within a group. Instead of tracking every individual expense debt, EquiShare calculates the optimal way to settle all balances with the minimum number of transactions.

## How Settlements Work

### From Expenses to Settlements

As you add expenses, EquiShare tracks each person's balance:

- **Positive balance**: This person is owed money (creditor)
- **Negative balance**: This person owes money (debtor)
- **Zero balance**: This person is settled up

<Info>
  Settlements are automatically recalculated every time an expense is added, deleted, or when a settlement is marked as paid.
</Info>

### Example: How Balances Build Up

```
Expense 1: ₹900 dinner paid by Alice, split equally among Alice, Bob, Carol
- Alice: +₹900 - ₹300 = +₹600
- Bob: -₹300
- Carol: -₹300

Expense 2: ₹600 taxi paid by Bob, split equally among Alice, Bob, Carol
- Alice: +₹600 - ₹200 = +₹400 total
- Bob: -₹300 + ₹600 - ₹200 = +₹100 total
- Carol: -₹300 - ₹200 = -₹500 total

Resulting settlements:
- Carol pays Alice ₹400
- Carol pays Bob ₹100
```

## The Minimal Transaction Algorithm

EquiShare uses a debt simplification algorithm to minimize the number of payments needed.

### How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Calculate Net Balances">
    Determine each person's final balance by summing all expenses and payments.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Identify Creditors and Debtors">
    Separate people into those owed money (positive balance) and those who owe (negative balance).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Match Largest Amounts">
    Start with the person who owes the most and the person who is owed the most.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Optimal Settlements">
    Match debtors to creditors, creating the minimum number of transactions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Why This Matters

**Without optimization**:
```
5 people, 10 expenses = potentially 45 settlements
```

**With minimal transaction algorithm**:
```
5 people, 10 expenses = typically 3-4 settlements
```

<Tip>
  The algorithm can reduce dozens of person-to-person transactions down to just a handful of payments, making settlement much simpler.
</Tip>

## Viewing Settlements

The Settlements section shows:

### Active Settlements

These are outstanding debts that need to be paid:

- **Orange/Red background**: Unpaid settlements
- **From** → **To**: Direction of payment
- **Amount**: How much needs to be paid
- **Settle button**: Mark as paid when completed

### Settled Transactions

View payment history by toggling "Show Settled":

- **Green background**: Already paid settlements
- **Checkmark badge**: Indicates completion
- Historical record of all payments

<Note>
  You can toggle between showing only active settlements or including settled ones using the "Show Settled" switch.
</Note>

## Marking Settlements as Paid

When someone makes a payment:

### Single Settlement

1. Find the settlement in the list
2. Click the **Settle** button
3. The settlement is marked as paid
4. Balances are recalculated
5. New optimal settlements are generated for remaining debts

<Info>
  Marking a settlement as paid doesn't delete it - it moves to the "Settled" history so you have a complete payment record.
</Info>

### Settle All

If everyone has paid their debts:

1. Click the **Settle All** button
2. Confirm the action
3. All active settlements are marked as paid
4. The group shows as "All Settled Up"

<Warning>
  Only use "Settle All" when all debts have actually been paid. This action marks all outstanding settlements as complete.
</Warning>

## Balance Calculation

Understand how your balance is calculated:

### For Each Person

1. **Start at zero**
2. **Add**: All expenses they paid
3. **Subtract**: All expense shares they owe
4. **Add**: Settlements they've paid to others
5. **Subtract**: Settlements others have paid to them

### Rounding and Precision

The system handles precision carefully:

- Amounts are rounded to 2 decimal places (paisa)
- Balances within ₹0.01 are considered settled
- Settlements below ₹0.01 are not created
- Auto-balance handles minor rounding differences

## Settlement Workflows

### End of Month Settlement

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Settlements">
    Check the Settlements section to see who owes whom.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make Payments">
    Transfer money via your preferred payment method (UPI, bank transfer, cash).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Mark as Paid">
    As each payment is made, click Settle to mark it complete.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify Balances">
    Check that the remaining settlements match any unpaid debts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Immediate Settlement

For groups that settle up after each expense:

1. Add the expense to the group
2. Check the updated settlements
3. Make payment immediately
4. Mark the settlement as paid right away
5. Group returns to "All Settled Up" state

### Final Settlement (Closing a Group)

When ending a group:

1. Ensure all expenses are entered
2. Export data for records
3. Review all settlements
4. Make all outstanding payments
5. Click "Settle All" once everything is paid
6. Export final settled data
7. Consider deleting the group

## Troubleshooting Settlements

### Settlement Doesn't Match Expectations

If settlements seem incorrect:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Check All Expenses">
    - Review each expense in the group
    - Verify amounts and splits are correct
    - Look for missing or duplicate expenses
    - Check that the right people are included in each split
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Review Settled History">
    - Toggle "Show Settled" to see past payments
    - Ensure previous settlements were marked correctly
    - Check for settlements marked as paid by mistake
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Understand the Algorithm">
    - The optimal path may not be intuitive
    - Person A might pay Person B even if A paid B's expenses directly
    - This is normal - the algorithm optimizes globally, not per-expense
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Small Rounding Differences

You might see very small amounts (like ₹0.01):

- These occur due to floating-point math
- Settlements below ₹0.01 are automatically ignored
- Use Auto Balance when creating expenses to minimize rounding
- These don't indicate errors, just normal computer math

### "All Settled Up" but Someone Still Owes

If the system shows settled but you know there's debt:

1. Check if all expenses were entered
2. Verify no expenses were accidentally deleted
3. Look at settled history - might be marked paid incorrectly
4. Consider if there's an external debt not tracked in EquiShare

## Best Practices

### Regular Settlement

<Tip>
  Settle up regularly (weekly or monthly) rather than letting debts accumulate. This makes payments smaller and more manageable.
</Tip>

### Clear Communication

When settling:

- Coordinate payment method beforehand
- Confirm receipt before marking as settled
- Include payment reference in transaction message
- Screenshot settlements before paying for reference

### Record Keeping

- Export data before settling all debts
- Keep export files for your records
- Note payment methods used (useful for tax purposes)
- Archive settled group exports

### Payment Methods

EquiShare doesn't process payments - you arrange payment yourself:

- **UPI**: Quick and convenient for small amounts
- **Bank Transfer**: Better for larger settlements
- **Cash**: Fine for in-person settlements
- **Payment Apps**: Venmo, PayPal, etc. work great

<Warning>
  Always mark settlements as paid only AFTER you've received or made the actual payment. Marking early can cause confusion.
</Warning>

## Understanding "All Settled Up"

When you see the "All Settled Up" message:

### What It Means

- All current expenses are balanced
- No active settlements exist
- Everyone has paid their share
- The group balance is zero

### When You'll See It

- Immediately after creating a new group (no expenses yet)
- After settling all outstanding debts
- When expenses perfectly balance (rare)

### What It Doesn't Mean

- It doesn't mean the group is closed
- You can still add expenses
- Historical data is still preserved
- The group continues to exist

## Advanced Scenarios

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Complex Multi-Person Settlements">
    **Scenario**: 6 people with multiple expenses
    
    **How it works**:
    - System calculates net balance for each person
    - Algorithm finds minimal transactions (usually 4-5 for 6 people)
    - Payments may seem indirect but are mathematically optimal
    - Trust the algorithm - it minimizes total transfers
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Partial Settlements">
    **Scenario**: Someone pays part of what they owe
    
    **Solution**:
    - Add the partial payment as a new expense
    - Mark the person as payer, the creditor as sole participant
    - Enter the partial amount paid
    - System recalculates remaining balance automatically
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Person Leaves Group Mid-Settlement">
    **Scenario**: Someone needs to settle and leave before the group ends
    
    **Solution**:
    - Check their current settlements
    - Have them pay their debts
    - Mark those settlements as paid
    - They can leave the group (if member, not owner)
    - Their historical expenses remain but they're out of future ones
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
